SINGAPORE IMMIGRATION AND VISA CONSULTANCY SERVICES FOR INDIANS

Singapore has a fairly large number of MNCs (Multinational companies.) This, in turn, has fuelled the growth of this island-nation which is also one of the four Asian tigers. This city-state has a very stable economy and provides various opportunities for research and specialized jobs. Further, from a job perspective, acquiring work experience from one of the fast-paced startups or MNCs of Singapore can aggrandize your resume.

Singapore’s government has set up the tax system to maximize business investment. The tax levied on businesses is quite low. Further, the income tax is a lot lower than that of most other developed nations. Singapore also has a booming IT sector.

Of course, there is much more to life than work. A multicultural ambiance makes it easier for new immigrants to integrate into the society. Still, the majority of people have their ethnic backgrounds as Indians, Chinese or Malays. There is no paucity of entertainment options in Singapore. Adventure parks, shopping malls, and luxurious hotels are more than enough to kindle a spark in your life.

English is the official language of Singapore. Hence, you won’t have to learn a new language to live, work or study in this country.

Various dependent visa categories and family nomination visas are also available. The applicant will require documents proving the relationship with the dependents.

ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA

ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA FOR SINGAPORE IMMIGRATION VISA FOR INDIANS

For most visa categories, you need to fulfill the conditions mentioned below:

  • Valid passport that won’t expire for the next 6 months
  • Return ticket if applicable for your visa category
  • Documents to prove all information that you entered in Singapore visa application form.
  • Funds to support self for the first few years in Singapore
  • If you’ve been nominated by an employer or family member, then you must provide documents supporting the nomination.
  • Documents proving your qualification and relationship with the person or organization that nominated you.
  • Character and health certificates
