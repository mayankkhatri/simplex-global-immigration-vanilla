GERMANY VISA TYPES

TYPES OF GERMANY IMMIGRATION VISAS TO APPLY
  1. Germany Tourist or visitor visa: This is a visa for people that want to travel to Germany for sightseeing or visiting family or friends.
  2. Germany Job seeker visa: If you are planning to go to Germany and look for a job this is the visa for which you have to apply. It is also known as a jobs visa.
  3. Germany Working visa: If you have already got a job in Germany, this is the visa you need.
  4. Business visa: If you have business related work in Germany for more than 90 days in 6 months you can apply for this visa.
  5. Training or internship visa: This is a visa for people from third-world countries to attend a training or an internship in Germany.
  6. Medical treatment visa: If you have an illness and wish to seek treatment in Germany then this visa is for you.
  7. Germany Schengen visa: The most frequent visitors of Schengen, Germany remain to be Indians. During the year 2017, 1.3 million Indians visited Europe, and out of this number, approximately 156,900 applied for Germany visas and most wanted to visit Germany when they were interviewed.

VISA APPLICATION PROCESS

VISA APPLICATION PROCESS FOR GERMANY FROM INDIA

After you have completed your application form, you will have to make an appointment with the German visa application centre. Once there you have to ensure all your documents are stacked in the order mentioned in the checklist and that you have completed and signed your form. The papers and application you submit will then be verified. If your application is incomplete or a document is missing, this whole process will be restarted.

Once your papers have been checked, you will receive a printed copy of your application that you will sign and will then be required to pay for the visa. Once this is done your biometric details will be collected. This is the last step in the process, and you will then have to wait to hear back from the Consulate when the visa is processed.

Q. DOES GERMANY PROVIDES PR?

A. This permit is mostly given to people after they have held the temporary residence permit for a few years or for those with the EU Blue Card. They need to prove that they have worked for at least 5 years, that their job was approved by the Federal Employment Agency.

Q. WHAT IS GERMANY JOB SEEKER VISA?

A. The job seeker visa for Germany is a Long-Term Residency Permit, which allows you to stay in the country for six months and look for a job.

Q. HOW MANY DAYS TAKE TO PROCESS OF APPLICATIONS FOR GERMANY VISA FOR INDIANS?

Q. WHAT ARE THE MOST POPULAR VISA TYPES FOR GERMANY?

A. The most popular visa types in Germany for Indians are below:

  2. National visa (for long-stay visits)

Q. IS GERMANY PERMANENT RESIDENCY (PR) EASY?

A. It is easy to get PR in Germany. You can apply for German PR if you have lived in Germany for five consecutive years. You should be paying taxes to the German government in other words you are employed in Germany.

Q. CAN I MOVE TO GERMANY WITHOUT A VISA?

A. You are not allowed to work in Germany without a valid work visa. US and Canadian citizens are allowed to remain in Germany for 90 days without a visa (but aren't allowed to have a job). You can apply for a residence/work visa after arriving in Germany but do that well before your 90 days expire.

Q. CAN I WORK WITH GERMAN STUDENT VISA?

A. It is legal to work in Germany with a student visa, but students are restricted to the number of days they can work. This is 120 full days each year or 240 half days. Students from the EU can work for up to 20 hours per week without the need for a work permit.
