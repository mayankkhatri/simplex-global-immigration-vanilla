Working in Sweden

You need a work permit if you are a non-EU national and want to work in Sweden. Sweden is the fourth European country that has launched a Job Seeker visa to address labour shortages. It means you can now go to Sweden without an offer of employment before arrival.

Sweden unveiled the job seeker visa in April 2022, and in addition, it also determined the process for prospective immigrants seeking to move to Sweden without a job offer.

With this new accomplishment, Sweden joins Germany, Austria, and Portugal among the European countries that launched the job seeker visa due to the massive shortage of workers.

If you want to apply in Sweden then a work permit or a Job seeker visa is the simplest route in case you are a non EU overseas national who is interested in working here. You can do this via online as applications which are submitted online are send straight to the Swedish Migration Agency and have the facility of a priority processing.

There are many work opportunities in many cities in Sweden which are Stockholm, Gothenburg and Malmö. Knowing about the official language can be a great incentive when you are working in Sweden as it can help you land a job.

On weekends you can go and move around 30 national parks or reside overnight in the global original Icehotel, situated in the village of Jukkasjärvi. Most of the Sweden relies mostly on overseas trade and is known for their excellent working conditions and has the most resilient job markets in the world.

Sweden Job Seeker Visa- Why is it Important?

Sweden has a shortage of workers with specific skills across many sectors. There is a lack of well-trained nurses, doctors, engineers, teachers, carpenters, electricians, mechanics, and more. The shortage is expected to exist over the next five to ten years.

Job agencies in Sweden have urged Swedish employers to consider recruiting workers with limited skills to fill rising job vacancies, as recent reports show a constant labour shortage.

Sweden was experiencing a shortage even before the pandemic hit the world. The country desperately needs qualified chefs, system developers, assistants, healthcare professionals, etc. The need intensified as the market started up again. The nation is looking for innovative and talented working professionals which is why they have launched with the Sweden Job Seeker visa.

Many employers are struggling to hire workers for entry-level positions, such as restaurant waiting staff. The primary reason is that restaurant workers got laid off amid the pandemic. Many of them have found jobs in different sectors.

The Job Seeker visa is valid for a period of 3 months but can be extended till 9 months also. Those who are qualified in jobs are issued the Sweden Job Seeker visa. Which is why when you need to apply for this visa you need an advanced degree.

The Swedish Migration Agency directly sends your documents so that your level of education can be evaluated with the authorities. The agency cannot ask questions prior to making your application in case of Sweden Job Seeker visa.

With the increasing demand for skilled professionals in Sweden, the job seeker visa is an excellent opportunity for non-EU citizens who do not wish to immigrate to Sweden through education or business investment. You can migrate for a specific duration, search for jobs, or start a business.

What are the eligibility criteria?

To qualify for a job seeker visa for Sweden, you need the following:

  • Must have completed studies equivalent to an advanced level degree.
  • Be able to include a Letter of Consent that gives the Swedish Council for Higher Education the right to contact higher educational authorities in your home country to verify your documents.
  • You must have sufficient funds to manage your expenses during the period you are applying for a permit.
  • You must demonstrate a well-defined plan to seek employment or explore business opportunities in Sweden.
  • You must have health insurance valid for care in Sweden.
  • It would help if you were currently living outside of Sweden.
  • You must have a valid passport.

Duration

Once you apply, the Swedish Migration Agency will review your documents and decide your case. If all documents are satisfactory, you will be granted a permit for at least three months for no more than nine months.

If the permit is issued for over three months, you will receive a residence permit card. The card is evidence that your stay is valid in Sweden and features your fingerprint, photo, and other relevant information related to your visa.

What if I have secured a job offer in Sweden or started a business?

If you land a job in Sweden during your stay, you can apply for a work permit. Please note that you cannot start working immediately in Sweden before applying to the Swedish Migration Agency for a valid work permit.

If you want to launch a business, then a residence permit under a self-employed person is the visa you should apply for.
